Early life     1990-2010

Until i moved to brighton for university, i had only ever lived in one house, which was my parents home in weset london With both my parents having polish ancestry my first language was naturally polish, and only learned english when i went to elementary school. Sadly my polish never recovered from being dethroned as my native language although I am still conversationally fluent, and have taunt myself to read polish to some extent.


My first school was called Hill House and it was spread out in sloan square and knightsbridge. This was where is spent my time untill my 13+ exams, which i skipped as i had already gotten a place at my secondary school, City of London Boys School.


City of London was where I took my gcses and a levels. My gcses included all the required modules with russian as my chosen language. For my as levels i took Maths Physics Art and Philosophy, dropping the latter for my a levels.


After my a levels i took some time to work in various locations, namely currys and after an extreamly informative time working as a chef. Initially i thought to take a degree in architecture, but instead decided to follow into phsyics instead, and found a place at sussex university in brighton.

University     2010-2015

At sussex I developed many of the skills i have today. I greatly enjoyed my degree and all the courses in it


While working through my studies i took part in both student council and took charge of the physics society at sussex. this gave me ample experience organising a multitude of events and coordinating people to pre assigned tasks.


Once my undergraduate had been completed i decided i wanted to do further study, and as my interest up until that point had always been in the more theoretical subjects, i chose to find a theoretical particle physics project. At the time the higgs had not yet been found and supersymmetry was still a hopeful theory with results possibly coming in within the next yeat. This changed during my masters and it is quite clear that if supersymmetry exists, it is not how it was outlined in my paper.


One thing that stood out to me was how disconnected i felt to the subject matter, and as such decided to not follow on to a phd in theoretical physics. i decided to look for something more practical after i left.

Post-University     2015-2019

My initial desire was to try to find more details on the emerging technology of quantum computation. Finding a phd opportunity or really any sort of professional employment in the area proved challanging with my background. Thus i endevoured to keep working in laboratories and try to develop the skills needed to research this area.


This led me back to london where i first found employment as a chef for a few hectic months in summer. After this i found steady employment as a science technician at 2 different schools. While these jobs were enjoyable and gave me a lot of experience with health and safety in laboratories, i still wanted to return to university to finish up my studies. By now machine learning had taken off in the public zyteguist and left me with some interesting questions about its applications in quantum computing.


I decided to apply for a masters in quantum communication at UCL with a focus on using machine learning on quantum systems.

University 2.0     2019-PRESENT

While my last masters left me with only theoretical skills, this one gave me a fantastic opportunity to practice my coding ability. I had always enjoyed the coding i had done, but it was quite limited in its scope. I had the opportunity here to really spread my wings with the problems and projects i took on.


In the first term i had to find my own project supervisor as the university was overbooked, i found one that was willing to take me on with a project that involved using machine learning techniquest on quatum systems. I also found two more courses that taught me about high performance computing and general coding pratices such as github.


I learned that the things i wanted to achieve in machine learning were completely doable without the use of qubits, although i had the ability to extend these techniques to them if they became commercially viable in the future.


Since finishing my degree i have been spending my days coding and learning new subjects while looking for employment.
