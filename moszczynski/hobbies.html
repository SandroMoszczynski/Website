Cooking

Most of the chefs I've met or read about have stories of how they cooking with a parental figure from a young age, which nurtured their interest in the subject. My interest came about from an almost opposite direction. I have memories of making cakes when I was younger but my interest really started when I started cooking meals for myself.


This interest was given a golden opportunity shortyly before university when I found a job as a kitchen porter at a north london tratorie. The hours were long and the journey took over an hour but it really fed into my passion as I was given the opportunity to assist in preperation and eventually took on a role more closely resembelling a commis chef.


However I eventually decided to keep with my decision to go to university instead of the culinary arts, although my interest in the subject remained and I spent the next few years developing more skills. Since then I have had a far less enjoyable stint as a chef but my enthusiasm for cooking has not deminised.


I enjoy throwing semi lavish dinner parties for friends and self catering social events I organise. This is in addition to my much maligned determination to experiment with dishes. I can recall a specific time I forced myself and my partner to each ham hocks for about two weeks while I perfected a recipie.

Computers

This was again a surprisingly late addition to my interests. My parents were never particularly computer literate and we only owned the one pc for many years. The first pc I had was probably in about 2004, at around the same time I got my first console, the PS2. I used the computer to run World of Warcraft, which it did not do a good job of.


Thus started my interest in computers, or rather, the search to be able to play my games at a decent framerate. I bought a computer from a colleague while I worked at currys and this served me well for a while. A few upgrades and assistant from friends got me through untill university when I built my first computer.


This computer lasted me for many years, although with notable additions of a full water loop after some not so educated choices in gpu. The image opposite is one of my misscalculations on my latest build, do not use coloured coolant, it will stain everything.

Gaming

As mentioned in the computer building section, I have a keen interest in games. My first memory of a game is probably playing the old space invaders on my fathers computer when he wasnt using it. It was acceced through command line, before desktops existed, yes I'm old enough to have used a save icon.


After this my next gaming related memory was playing pokemon when it came out, along with their sequels. Thus owning a gameboy and the GBA when it came out. This lead to my first RPG, golden sun and its sequel. After this I "tricked" my parents into buying me a playstation on a family vacation to Ghana, although at this time I still wasnt playing games that often. This would change when I started playing World of Warcraft, and is probably where I can trace my current hobby interest to.


Having my own computer allowed me to play much more frequently, and once I built my own functioning computer a few years later, I was free to really broaden my gaming horizons. These days I'm much more selective of the games that I play, valuing games that value my time to them. I.e. not requring playing long hours or tieing me into commitments every day, I have also made some great friends through the social groups that form naturally in these games.


Some of these online friends I also choose to see in person, through social events and boardgame nights. I am also currently a Dungeon Master in a D&D group and own many board games that I frequently whip out at said social events.

Sports

Despite my obviously nerdy interests I maintain a very good knowledge of physical sports aswell. This can mostly be attributed to the prep school I attended, Hill House. Instead of usual breaks and lunches, we instead did 2-3 hours a day of mandatory sports. We would often walk over to Hyde Park or the Duke of Yorks barracks to partake in a long list of sports. From this came a good grasp of a large range of rules and basic play techniques of various sports. Of particular interest for me were fencing and cricket, the latter of which I was on the school team for.At my secondary school my interest in sports diminished somewhat, but I did develop an interest in basketball.


It wasnt until university that I took up regular team sports again, joining a football team that met regularly and taking up squash with a few friends, as well as starting a fairly vigorous gym routine, that sadly I didnt keep up for as long as I should have.


Currently I try to run 2-3 days a week, and do other weights on those days as well. When I'm able I also like to play some squash with my partner.
